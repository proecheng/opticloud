# Commercial Buyer FAQ

## Who is OptiCloud for?

OptiCloud is aimed at teams that need optimization or forecasting workflows but do not want to build a solver platform from scratch.

## How do Credits work?

Credits are the pricing abstraction used by the product. Runtime billing behavior and refund handling follow the implemented billing service; M4.5 pricing copy is buyer framing, not a new billing engine.

## What happens when a run fails?

Failed-run and cancellation handling must follow billing and support stories. Buyer-facing copy should say that refund rules are documented in-product and avoid unsupported guarantees.

## What security posture can we share?

We can describe existing technical controls, data minimization, and planned legal review. We must not claim SOC 2, ISO 27001, AIGC filing approval, or production SLA completion unless a future evidence story verifies it.

## What data retention story can we tell?

Retention depends on data type. Reproducibility vouchers and image archival have their own runbooks and evidence boundaries. Customer inputs and commercial records must follow legal and privacy documents.

## Do you support academic and enterprise plans?

Academic and Enterprise are buyer segments in the roadmap. Academic onboarding exists as a documented path. Enterprise packaging requires procurement and legal review before any signed commitment.

## How should we compare with Gurobi?

Use conservative language: Gurobi is a known optimization standard, and OptiCloud should be evaluated by workflow fit, cost model, transparency, and pilot results. Benchmark claims belong to the separate M4.5b story.

## What support path exists?

Early commercial support is founder-led. Customer Success workflows define onboarding, follow-up, and escalation paths, but do not create production SLA promises in M4.5.
